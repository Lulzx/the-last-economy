The Premise

The Intelligence Inversion

For three hundred years, economics assumed one unchanging fact: human cognition is scarce. Rare expertise commands rare prices. The doctor, the lawyer, the engineer — each a high-value node in the network of civilization.

AI has broken this assumption completely. Not gradually. Not partially. The marginal cost of expert cognition has collapsed by 99.99% in under a decade. Our economic models — built entirely on the scarcity of mind — have no framework for what happens next.

This is not a productivity improvement. This is a phase transition. The kind that reshapes civilizations. The Last Economy is an engineering manual for what must be built on the other side.

"We are not approaching post-scarcity in the domain of intelligence; we have arrived. A single AI can now write a million legal briefs, create a billion architectural plans, or compose infinite symphonies — at zero marginal cost. This breaks the fundamental equation of economics entirely."
— The Last Economy, Chapter 3

First Law

Flow

Value must be conserved and circulated. A system that hoards is a system that stops learning. Entropy always wins against accumulation.

Second Law

Openness

The system must remain open to new energy and information to fight entropy and avoid model collapse. Closed systems decay.

Third Law

Resilience

The system must maintain a diversity of components and strategies to survive unforeseen shocks. Monocultures are catastrophically fragile.

Key Ideas

A New Science of Value

01  ·  Foundation

The MIND Dashboard

GDP is criminally insane — it rewards destruction and ignores what matters. MIND measures four true capitals: Material, Intelligence, Network, and Diversity. Zero in any dimension and the entire system collapses.

M × I × N × D

02  ·  Architecture

The Three Flows

Smith, Marx, and Hayek were each right — about different parts of the same river. Gradient flows (scarce atoms), Circular flows (abundant bits), and Harmonic flows (trust and institutions) must all be understood together.

Hodge Decomposition

03  ·  Contract

The New Social Contract

The old contract — labor for security — is broken. The new one must be an endowment: Dignity (access to intelligence), Capability (sovereign AI), and Viability (knowledge commons). Not charity. Physics.

UAI — Universal Access to Intelligence

04  ·  Role

Humanity as the Alignment Layer

AI is the Action Layer: uncapped, infinitely scalable execution. Humanity is the Alignment Layer: the source of values, ethics, and ultimate purpose. This is not a partnership of equals — it is a symbiosis of different intelligences.

The Stakes

Three Possible Crystals

Civilizations don't evolve gradually — they crystallize. Like a supercooled liquid that snaps into one of a finite number of stable structures, our civilization will solidify into one of exactly three configurations. The physics allows no others.

Future One

Digital Feudalism

The Default Path · The Path of Inaction

AI controlled by a handful of corporations. Humans receive UBI in exchange for data and attention. Not citizens — users. Not workers — content. Medieval power dynamics restored at planetary scale, with algorithms instead of lords.

Default Trajectory

Future Two

The Great Fragmentation

The Fear Response · Balkanization

Nations erect walls around their AI systems. Algorithmic cold war. Each bloc optimizes against the others. The global commons of knowledge dissolves into competing silos, each getting weaker as cooperation collapses.

Fear Response

Future Three

Human Symbiosis

The Conscious Choice · The Hard Path

Intelligence as commons. AI amplifying human purpose rather than replacing it. A civilization where abundance is a feature, not a threat — and the liberation from necessity becomes the beginning of genuine human flourishing.
